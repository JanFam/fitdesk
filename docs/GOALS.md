# FitDesk — Goals

## What We're Building

FitDesk is a client management platform for solo online fitness coaches managing 5–50 clients. It replaces the chaos of disconnected tools (Google Sheets, WhatsApp, PayPal, Calendly, Google Drive) with one purpose-built workspace: coaches assign workouts, clients log progress and submit weekly check-ins, and everything from messaging to billing lives in one place.

## The Problem

Solo online fitness coaches are currently duct-taping together 5+ free/cheap tools to run their business. Existing tools (Trainerize, TrueCoach) are built for gyms and priced accordingly ($99+/mo). The market is 1M+ solo coaches in the US alone, growing at 15.6% annually. The pricing gap between "free spreadsheet" and "$99+/mo gym software" is exactly where a $29–79/mo solo-coach tool sits.

## Target Customer

Solo online fitness coaches:
- 5–50 clients
- Mid-career (2–5 years in)
- Running their business solo (not at a gym)
- Frustrated with admin work consuming time they'd rather spend coaching

## Target Market

- US solo fitness coaches: 1M+ (growing 15.6% annually)
- Willing to pay: $29–79/mo for the right tool
- Primary acquisition: content marketing (TikTok/YouTube)

## Core Value Proposition

Replace 5 disconnected tools with one workspace built for solo coaches. No enterprise price tag, no hidden fees, no scale ceiling.

## Revenue Model

Tiered subscription:
- Starter: $29/mo (up to 10 clients)
- Pro: $49/mo (up to 30 clients)
- Scale: $79/mo (up to 50 clients)

## Current Stage

**Pre-product.** Currently validating demand via waitlist landing page before writing code.

## Key Risks

1. **Customer research gap**: Creator hasn't done 20 customer interviews yet — this is the critical validation step before building
2. **Demand uncertainty**: "Coaches complain publicly" ≠ "coaches will pay $39/mo" — waitlist validates interest, not willingness to pay
3. **Content marketing dependency**: Organic social is the primary acquisition channel — untested for this specific audience
